01

The moment

Youth sports is huge, emotional, fragmented, and still under-operated.

Families already spend like sports is infrastructure. But most operators still run with patched-together payments, texts, spreadsheets, gym rentals, and unstructured coaching data. VAI turns the facility into the software platform.

$30B+Annual family spend

Axios reports U.S. families spend more than $30B annually on youth sports.

58%Participation

Project Play reports U.S. ages 6-17 participation rose to 58% in 2024.

$1,016Primary sport

30MAthletes

L.E.K. cites roughly 30M organized youth sports participants annually.

The market is mature enough to scale, but fragmented enough for an operating system to matter.

The existing ZDI deck is not just a local story. It is proof that a well-run complex can aggregate leagues, tournaments, club management, training, sublease income, and athlete development into a repeatable operating engine.

From local sports facility to AI-powered youth sports network.

VAI does not replace the gym. VAI makes the gym intelligent, connected, and monetizable across every stakeholder: operator, director, coach, athlete, parent, sponsor, recruiter, and community.

A visible local destination: courts, fields, camps, leagues, showcases, training, tournaments, and community events.

Operating layer

AI operator

The complex brings families into the VAI ecosystem. Subscriptions keep them there between practices, games, seasons, and locations.

07

Athlete + family upside

The family does not just spend. They can earn, save, and progress.

Youth sports has a cost problem. The VAI model should be designed with a family value return: better organization, scholarship pathways, earned credits, safe media monetization, and local work opportunities.

Referral credits

Families earn VAI credits for bringing teams, camps, tournaments, and sponsors into the ecosystem.

Scholarship pool

Allocate 1-2% of sponsorship or gross program revenue into need-based participation credits.

Guardian-approved media

Highlight reels and sponsor placements can fund athlete profiles or training credits, with strict minor-safety controls.

Teen leadership track

Paid scorekeeping, officiating support, event operations, content tagging, and junior coaching roles.

VAI rewards wallet

Credits redeemable toward camps, clinics, subscriptions, gear, concessions, or tournament fees.

10

Why VAI wins

The moat is the operating system plus the local complex.

Competitors can rent gyms. Software companies can sell tools. VAI combines venue, software, AI, family identity, media, and development into one loop.

  • AData moat.Rosters, schedules, performance, payments, attendance, standings, waivers, communications, media, and development goals become one longitudinal athlete graph.
  • BDistribution moat.Every league, event, camp, and team adds app users and family relationships without pure paid acquisition.
  • COperational moat.AVANTI makes best practices repeatable: reminders, fill rates, registration health, schedule conflict detection, and revenue suggestions.
  • DBrand moat.The physical complex makes VAI tangible locally, while public follow pages and invite links make it visible digitally.

12

Deal structure + ask

Fund the ZDI purchase, merge VAI into NewCo, then scale the platform.

The concept is a combined operating company: acquire the proven local sports business, contribute/merge the VAI technology stack, and capitalize the new entity to expand locations while continuing to build the software moat.

1. Purchase ZDIFund acquisition of the existing ZDI business, operating contracts, local relationships, event engine, facility model, and proven revenue base.
2. Merge VAI into NewCoContribute the VAI App, VAI Game OS, AVANTI, VAI+, VAI Mentor, brand, product roadmap, and AI/data architecture into the combined company.
3. Fund expansionCapitalize the pilot-to-network rollout: site diligence, leasehold improvements, equipment, staff, launch events, working capital, and regional shared services.
4. Continue VAI developmentInvest in Game OS hardening, AVANTI workflows, payments/reporting, family app growth loops, Mentor content, media tools, security, and data safety.

Next diligence step: define NewCo ownership, purchase price, VAI contribution value, governance, founder/operator roles, and a source-of-funds / use-of-funds model for acquisition, expansion, and product development.

14

Diligence notes

Key assumptions to prove before financing.

The opportunity is powerful because the local proof is real and the software wedge is differentiated. The diligence needs to be equally serious.

  • 1Facility model.Lease, buy, or partner? Each changes capex, EBITDA, control, speed, and risk.
  • 2Conversion math.Validate VAI App activation, VAI+ attach, Mentor attach, and churn by cohort.
  • 3Director supply.Confirm acquisition pipeline for leagues, clubs, trainers, events, and school partners.
  • 4Minor safety.Guardian controls, media rights, privacy, payment compliance, and youth employment rules must be first-class.
  • 5Replicability.Codify the exact playbook that lets location two open faster and cleaner than location one.
